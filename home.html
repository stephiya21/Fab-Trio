First Training Class

A personal trainer's main job is to create a safe and effective exercise program just for you. Your program should be unique to you and your fitness goals. For example, maybe a high-intensity workout routine will help you reach your goals fast. But you have that bum shoulder and your range of motion isn't awesome. A certified personal trainer will help increase your level of fitness without causing additional harm.

Second Training Class

If you're bad about stretching after your typical routine, adding a second workout focused on recovery and mobility may be a good option. Your first session can incorporate your typical, heavy training, whether you strength train, do more intense cardio, or high-intensity interval work.Then later in the day, you can add a recovery workout consisting of low-intensity cardio, yoga, stretching, or foam rolling. Remember, recovery is just as important as strenuous exercise and will help reduce the likelihood of injuries.

Third Training Class

Stage 3 holds up to 10 metabolic resistance classes per day with every class completing the same workout. For example, a class on Monday at 5:00 AM will be doing the same workout as a class on Monday at 6:30 PM.Every daily workout will be different. We believe that providing you with a new workout each time you come in encourages greater involvement and prevents plateauing. Consistency is key with our program, so we recommend that you come at least 2-3 times a week.

Fourth Training Class

With the right workout routine, you can achieve a whole lot in just four weeks. This 16-workout plan is proof of that. The 16 workouts it contains are broken down by areas of the body and though the emphasis is on your chest, back and arms, no muscle goes without its turn in the spotlight over the course of each week. The plan is designed to push your muscles beyond their comfort zone so your body rebuilds them bigger and stronger
